Frequently Asked Questions

1Why not just use Social Media?

Social media is great, however, you do loose control over the data you give them. When images are uploaded to social media sites they are often compressed and even resized or cropped. Not everyone uses the same social media apps. Having your event photos strewn across multiple sites and apps makes collecting your (now cropped and compressed) images difficult, if not impossible.

2 When I pay for "an Event" what do I get?

An event will be available to you for 14 days from the time of your first upload. 14 days allows time to set things up, to test things out and also a buffer after the event so that people who maybe weren't aware you were using Everafter can still have a chance to share their photos with you, even long after the event has ended.

Included with every Everafter event is the ability for anyone at your event to upload files directly to the Live Feed. Also included is an admin panel visible only to the registered user (the person who paid for the Everafter event) that allows for image moderation, QR code generation, and a one click download (for downloading the entirity of the event photos)

3What if someone uploads something... inapropriate?

People will be people and this is bound to happen. Everafter however does allow the registered user to monitor and delete images immediately. Deleted files will be removed from the live feed and moved to a "Deleted" folder. Those deleted files will also be included in your one click download.

4 How long can I use it?

If you have paid for an event it is activated when you upload your first image and remains activated for 14 days

5 How long do you store my images?

Images uploaded to Everafter are stored for 14 days. The first day begins when the first image is uploaded.

6 How do I send the live feed to my big screen device?

There are a few different ways to do this



HDMI & USB Cabling

The simplest is probably using an HDMI or USB cable directly from your device to the big screen. While foolproof, this method does have it's drawbacks as your device now has to stay within the cable's length from the TV. Not ideal, but it works.



Chromecast

Required Transmitter: Any Chrome Browser or Android device
Required Receiver: Google Chromecast or Android TV device

Arguably the best (and definitely the most accessible and reliable) method of screen mirroring is Google Cast. This protocol works with almost any modern Android device, making it an extremely accessible solution. You’ll also need an Android TV or Chromecast device as a receiver, but considering that Chromecasts start at $35, it’s one of the more affordable solutions. There are also a fair number of smart TVs powered by Android TV. For instructions of casting with a chromecast click here



Apple AirPlay

Required Transmitter: iOS device or macOS computer
Required Receiver: Apple TV or Apple TV 4K

AWhile it’s neither as accessible nor as ironclad as Google Cast, it’s a decent solution. To use AirPlay, start with an iPhone, iPad or MacBook close at hand, and an Apple TV hooked up to your television. Full and current instructions are available from Apple here
